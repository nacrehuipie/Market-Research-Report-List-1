<p><p>Multi-layering chip inductors can be categorized into two main types based on their core materials: ceramic core and magnetic core. Ceramic core multi-layering chip inductors utilize a non-magnetic ceramic substrate, offering high stability and low loss, making them ideal for high-frequency applications. In contrast, magnetic core multi-layering chip inductors use a ferromagnetic material to enhance inductance and provide better performance at lower frequencies. Each type serves distinct applications in electronics, influencing design choices based on performance requirements.</p></p>
