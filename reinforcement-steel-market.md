<p><p>The reinforcement steel market primarily consists of deformed steel and mild steel types. Deformed steel, characterized by surface grooves or patterns, provides enhanced bonding with concrete, making it ideal for high-stress construction applications. In contrast, mild steel, known for its ductility and flexibility, is utilized in lower-stress scenarios and for general-purpose construction, such as residential buildings. Both types serve critical roles in ensuring structural integrity, with their selection depending on specific project requirements and load-bearing capacities.</p></p>
