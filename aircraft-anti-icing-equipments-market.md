<p><p>The aircraft anti-icing equipment market includes several systems designed to prevent ice buildup on critical surfaces. Electric pulse anti-icing systems use pulsed electric signals to disrupt ice formation. Liquid anti-icing systems spray heated fluids to melt ice on surfaces. Hot air anti-icing systems utilize heated air to prevent ice accumulation in areas like wings and engines. Electric heating anti-icing systems employ electrical resistance to generate heat, thereby preventing ice buildup, ensuring safety and efficiency during flight operations.</p></p>
