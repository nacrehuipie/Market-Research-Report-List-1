<p><p>Empty glass columns are essential in chromatography, primarily used for separating chemical mixtures. They come in various types, including packed columns and capillary columns. Packed columns contain a stationary phase packed with solid materials, optimizing separation efficiency but may require more maintenance. In contrast, capillary columns have a smaller diameter and utilize a thin film of stationary phase, offering higher resolution and faster analysis. Both column types cater to specific analytical needs in research and industrial applications.</p></p>
