<p><p>The high-temperature ceramics market includes various types, each serving distinct applications. Cordierite ceramics are known for their thermal shock resistance and are used in kiln furniture and automotive components. Alumina ceramics offer high strength and electrical insulation, making them popular in electronics and wear-resistant applications. Zirconia ceramics provide exceptional toughness and are favored in dental and cutting tools. Magnesium oxide ceramics exhibit high thermal stability and are utilized in furnace linings and insulators, catering to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
